Global Market Outlook - Q1 2025

The S&P 500 returned 4.2% in Q4 2024, bringing the full-year return to 25.0%. The rally was primarily driven by mega-cap technology stocks, with the Magnificent Seven contributing over 60% of the index return.

Interest Rate Environment: The Federal Reserve cut rates three times in 2024, bringing the federal funds rate to 4.25-4.50%. Markets are pricing in two additional cuts in 2025. The yield curve has normalized after its longest inversion in history.

Sector Performance: Technology led all sectors with a 36% return in 2024. Healthcare underperformed at 2.1%, weighed down by GLP-1 competition concerns and regulatory uncertainty. Energy declined 1.3% as oil prices remained range-bound between $68 and $78 per barrel.

Valuation Concerns: The S&P 500 forward P/E ratio stands at 22.4x, well above the 20-year average of 17.5x. However, earnings growth expectations of 14% for 2025 provide some justification for elevated multiples. The equal-weighted S&P 500 trades at a more reasonable 16.8x forward earnings.

Key Risks for 2025: Geopolitical tensions in Eastern Europe and the Middle East, potential trade policy changes from the new US administration, persistent inflation above the Fed's 2% target, and concentration risk in market-cap weighted indices.

Portfolio Strategy: Maintain diversified exposure across sectors and geographies. Consider increasing allocation to international developed markets, which trade at significant discounts to US equities. Fixed income offers attractive yields for the first time in years. Maintain a 5-10% cash allocation for opportunistic purchases during market pullbacks.
